Madras Beef Curry

Beef curry with adjustable heat!

This beef curry is a hit in our household. Chicken has easily become the number one go to protein for us, and while I am no stranger to eating the same thing over-and-over again, my wife rightly believes in variety. That's how we came to this beef curry! While Madras is not a traditional Indian curry, we found that this was easy for us to make a good product, something that we have struggled with in attempts to make traditional Indian recipes.

While Madras is not a traditional Indian curry, we enjoy this slow cooker version and hope you will too!

Ingredients

  • 750 g beef braising steak
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil

Marinade

  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin ground
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp fenugreek
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 tsp coriander
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp natural yoghurt

Sauce

  • 1 large onion
  • 1 2cm ginger piece
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • 100 ml boiling water
  • 400 g chopped tomatoes
  • 1 red chili pepper
  • 1 small handful curry leaves
  • 1 small handful of fresh coriander optional

Steps

  1. Start with the marinade, combining ingredients into a bowl.
  2. Remove any sinew from the beef, then cut into large chunks. Add the beef into the marinade bowl, making sure to coat the chunks. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Around 1-2 hours is ideal, but you can make ahead and prepare the night before!
  3. Once the beef has marinated long enough, heat a frying pan to high head, add the 1tbsp of vegetable oil, and sear the beef on all sides. Transfer to slow cooker when done.
  4. Add the remainder of the marinade paste from the bowl, along with the ginger, onion, tamarind paste, sugar, and beef stock that has dissolved in boiling water. pour the tomatoes in and add fresh red chili. Sprinkle in curry leaves and stir. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours.

Enjoy!
